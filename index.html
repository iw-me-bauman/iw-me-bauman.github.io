Members of the Bluffton community

Irwin Wiegner Bauman and Mary Ellen (Bonser) Bauman were members of the Bluffton community for over 50 years. Both were early graduates of Bluffton College and Irwin taught at the college from 1929 until 1966. This website documents their lives and contributions to their family, friends, and the college community.

Their stories

Irwin's early life

Irwin grew up in Sassamansville, Pennsylvania. His father was an entrepeneur whose most successful business was making and marketing Bauman's Apple Butter.

Mary's early life

Mary's father died when she was small and she grew up with foster parents when her mother was unable to care for her.

Marriage and life in Germany

Irwin and Mary were married in 1926 and in the fall of that year they set sail to Germany where Irwin began studies for his Ph.D.

Bluffton years

The Baumans moved to Bluffton in about 1930 when Irwin was hired as a professor at Bluffton College. They lived in Bluffton for the rest of their lives.
